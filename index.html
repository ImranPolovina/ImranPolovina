Manual testing

Bug report

When reporting a bug, we follow a specific format for the title and description to make it easy for the team to understand and reproduce the issue. The title begins with the feature in which the bug was found, followed by a brief summary of the problem. In the description, we leave a brief explanation and overview of the Bug we found and under what circumstances.

To ensure accurate and efficient reproduction, we provide each step without skipping any moves. We also include the expected and actual results to confirm the business logic and the root cause of the bug. Dev and Devices are here to provide information about the environment in which the Bug is created.

We require every bug report to include a video or picture, depending on the nature of the bug.

By providing a well-defined bug report, we make the process of resolving the issue much easier. Of course, we are open to modifying and improving these steps as needed to meet the team's requirements.

Daily report

To facilitate the team's workflow, I create a daily bug report at the end of each working day. This report outlines all the bugs we encountered during testing that day and is shared in a team chat where everyone has access. Each bug is linked to a ticket located in Jira, making it faster and simpler for the team to access each bug. By doing so, the team can review the defects we found and prioritize their work before the start of the next working day.
